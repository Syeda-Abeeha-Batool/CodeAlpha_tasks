What are the main differences between classical physics and quantum physics?
The main differences between classical physics and quantum physics lie in their foundational principles and the scale at which they operate. Classical physics, based on Newtonian mechanics, deals with macroscopic objects and follows deterministic laws of motion. Quantum physics, on the other hand, deals with microscopic particles and follows probabilistic behavior described by quantum mechanics, where particles can exist in multiple states simultaneously until observed.
Can you explain the concept of blockchain technology in simple terms?
Blockchain technology is a decentralized digital ledger system that records transactions across a network of computers. Each transaction is added to a "block" which is then linked to previous blocks, forming a chain of blocks, hence the term "blockchain." This technology ensures transparency, security, and immutability of transactions without the need for a central authority.

How do plants convert sunlight into energy?
Plants convert sunlight into energy through a process called photosynthesis. In photosynthesis, chlorophyll molecules in plant cells capture sunlight, which is then used to convert carbon dioxide (CO2) and water (H2O) into glucose (a form of sugar) and oxygen (O2). This process occurs in chloroplasts, the organelles responsible for photosynthesis in plant cells.
